Introducing Sequences

A Sequence is an arrangement of multiple processing tasks called Steps. Each Sequence is known by a friendly name, for example 'ProcessInvoices', which should in some way indicate the nature of the work that it's processing steps will perform.

The ESB framework will advance Messages through the Steps of a Sequence in the order you specify using drag and drop. A typical sequence will contain many steps. See diagram:

When building a new Sequence

  • Every Step in a Sequence has an associated Process, a numbered Position, and potentially a Config.
  • A Terminate process is automatically inserted into a new Sequence at the point that the Sequence is created. This Terminate step cannot be deleted.
  • It is the orchestrator's responsibility to ensure the Terminate Process is the final Step in every Sequence as any Steps after the Terminate Process will not be executed.

Detail Page Buttons

  • New Step will append a new Sequence step (containing your selected process). Once appended, you can drag and drop the step to reposition.
  • Enqueue Test Message enqueues a message into the sequence (referencing your user record as the underlying data entity esb__Id).
